Certificates of analysis

How to read the numbers above

Each result comes from Janoshik Analytical, an independent third-party lab — not from us. Here’s what each field means.

1Purity %

The share of the sample that is the target compound, measured by HPLC. Higher means fewer impurities — our standard is 99%+.

2Batch / Lot #

The unique ID for that production run. It links the certificate to the exact vial, so results are traceable.

3Test method

HPLC separates and quantifies the compound; mass spectrometry confirms its molecular weight and identity.

4Sample tested

The compound and label strength that was submitted to the lab for analysis.

5Measured content

The actual mass of compound found in the vial — confirming it meets or exceeds the labelled amount.

6Independent lab

Every result is issued by a third-party analytical lab, not by us — so the numbers are unbiased, and your vial’s own CoA ships with your order.

Research & authority

The science behind every compound

Peer-reviewed mechanisms, clinical-trial data, and primary literature for all 10 compounds. Tap any compound to read its full research profile.

Metabolic

GLP-3 Retatrutide

Mechanism of action

An investigational single-peptide unimolecular compound that simultaneously activates three GPCRs central to metabolism: the GLP-1, GIP, and glucagon receptors. GLP-1/GIP signaling stimulates glucose-dependent insulin secretion, delays gastric emptying, and suppresses appetite via hypothalamic POMC/CART pathways, while glucagon-receptor activation raises energy expenditure, hepatic mitochondrial activity, and lipid oxidation — addressing both energy intake and output.

Research focus

Studied in severe obesity, type-2 diabetes, metabolic dysfunction-associated steatotic liver disease (MASLD), and cardiovascular-risk models — notably for inducing weight loss at magnitudes previously achieved only with bariatric surgery.

Supporting clinical evidence

  • Phase 3 TRIUMPH-1: weekly 12 mg produced a 24.2% (26.2 kg) average body-weight reduction over 80 weeks vs 1.6% placebo; 26% of the 12 mg cohort lost ≥30%, and 100% fell below a BMI of 30.
  • Phase 3 TRANSCEND-T2D-1: 12 mg monotherapy lowered HbA1c by 2.1% over 40 weeks, bringing 72–82% of patients to normoglycemia (<5.7%) without severe hypoglycemia.
  • Tolerability concerns were mild-to-moderate gastrointestinal symptoms, occurring mainly during dose escalation.
Skin & Anti-Aging

GHK-Cu (Copper Tripeptide-1)

Mechanism of action

A naturally occurring copper-binding tripeptide (Glycyl-L-Histidyl-L-Lysine) that shields copper's redox activity while delivering it to metalloenzymes such as lysyl oxidase, superoxide dismutase, and cytochrome c oxidase. As a "matrikine" released during tissue injury, it acts as a chemoattractant for macrophages and endothelial cells and, at the genomic level, modulates roughly 4,000 human genes toward a regenerative state — stimulating collagen, elastin, and glycosaminoglycan synthesis and regulating MMP/TIMP balance.

Research focus

Impaired wound healing (ischemic, diabetic, and elderly tissue), radiation dermal damage, COPD, cellular senescence, and age-related skin deterioration, with additional interest in oncology gene-network modulation.

Supporting clinical evidence

  • Gene profiling using Broad Institute databases showed GHK-Cu downregulated aggressive metastatic genes (APP, LMNA, YWHAB) at nanomolar concentrations.
  • It reversed emphysematous gene signatures in lung fibroblasts via the TGF-β / integrin-β1 pathway, and restored p63 / integrin stemness markers in basal keratinocytes.
  • Placebo-controlled, split-face dermatology trials: GHK-Cu creams stimulated collagen, reduced fine and coarse wrinkles, and improved skin density and elasticity.
Recovery · Blend

Wolverine (BPC-157 / TB-500)

Mechanism of action

A synergistic stack of BPC-157 and TB-500. BPC-157, a 15-amino-acid peptide from gastric juice, accelerates neovascularization via the FAK/AKT/eNOS → VEGF cascade and drives organized type-I collagen deposition. TB-500 (a Thymosin β4 analog) acts systemically by sequestering monomeric G-actin to regulate cell migration, mobilize progenitor cells, and limit fibrotic scarring.

Research focus

Acute and chronic musculoskeletal injuries — tendon ruptures, ligament tears, muscle strains, post-surgical recovery — and mucosal cytoprotection of the intestinal lining.

Supporting clinical evidence

  • Preclinical models show improved functional, structural, and biomechanical outcomes in muscle, tendon, and ligament injury; chronic BPC-157 promoted bone-to-tendon junction repair.
  • A 2024 human pilot study of chronic, non-responsive knee pain found intra-articular BPC-157 gave long-term relief in 7 of 12 patients.
  • Thymosin β4 accelerated corneal repair in Phase II trials (RGN-259); 2025 intravenous safety evaluation confirmed BPC-157 was well-tolerated in healthy adults.
Cognitive

Semax

Mechanism of action

A synthetic heptapeptide (Met-Glu-His-Phe-Pro-Gly-Pro) conjugating an ACTH(4–7) fragment to Pro-Gly-Pro for metabolic stability without hormonal activity. It crosses the blood-brain barrier and upregulates BDNF and its TrkB receptor in the hippocampus, modulates dopamine and serotonin turnover, and alters the ischemic transcriptome — downregulating pro-inflammatory JNK signaling while raising survival factor pCREB and reducing MMP-9.

Research focus

Acute ischemic stroke recovery, transient ischemic attacks, vascular dementia and Alzheimer's-associated cognitive deficits, traumatic brain injury, and optic-nerve atrophy.

Supporting clinical evidence

  • Approved in Russia and Ukraine for ischemic stroke and vascular cognitive impairment.
  • In pMCAO stroke models, genome-wide profiling showed Semax altered 96 genes at 3 h and 68 at 24 h, promoting neovascularization and balancing post-ischemic neuroinflammation.
  • In APPswe/PS1dE9 Alzheimer's mice, intranasal Semax improved spatial learning and memory and significantly reduced amyloid-β plaques in the cortex and hippocampus.
Skin & Anti-Aging

Melanotan 2

Mechanism of action

A synthetic cyclic-lactam heptapeptide analog of α-melanocyte-stimulating hormone (α-MSH) and a non-selective agonist of the melanocortin receptors MC1, MC3, and MC4. MC1R engagement on dermal melanocytes drives UV-independent melanogenesis, while central MC3R/MC4R activation modulates energy balance, appetite, and sexual-function pathways.

Research focus

Skin pigmentation, psychogenic and organic erectile dysfunction, hypoactive sexual desire, and central appetite regulation.

Supporting clinical evidence

  • Double-blind, placebo-controlled crossover trials led by H. Wessells: a 0.025 mg/kg dose produced erections in 8 of 10 men with psychogenic ED, with 38 min of tip rigidity vs 3 min for placebo.
  • A follow-up study (organic-ED risk factors, n = 20) produced robust responses in 17 participants, with subjective sexual desire rising in 68% of active sessions vs 19% placebo.
  • Mc4r-knockout rat models confirmed the MC4R pathway's necessity; clinical development was later discontinued due to side effects (transient blood-pressure elevation, flushing, nausea in ~50%).
Growth · Blend

CJC-1295 / Ipamorelin

Mechanism of action

A dual growth-hormone-secretagogue strategy. CJC-1295, a GHRH analog whose DAC modification extends its half-life to ~5.8–8.1 days, raises GH amplitude through cAMP-dependent pituitary signaling. Ipamorelin, a highly selective GHS-R1a (ghrelin-receptor) agonist, raises GH pulse frequency via the Gαq–phospholipase-C cascade. Together they drive hepatic IGF-1 within physiological limits.

Research focus

Age-related somatopause, adult growth-hormone deficiency, muscle wasting, visceral adiposity, sleep architecture, and tissue recovery — without the supraphysiological spikes of exogenous HGH.

Supporting clinical evidence

  • In a randomized, double-blind, ascending-dose trial, a single 30–60 µg/kg dose of CJC-1295 produced a 2- to 10-fold rise in plasma GH for ≥6 days and a 1.5–3× IGF-1 elevation lasting 9–11 days.
  • Repeated dosing kept IGF-1 elevated for up to 28 days with no serious adverse reactions.
  • Ipamorelin is highly selective — it does not raise cortisol, prolactin, or ACTH; a Phase II trial (n = 117) confirmed good tolerability.
Skin & Anti-Aging · Blend

GLOW (GHK-Cu / BPC-157 / TB-500)

Mechanism of action

A tri-peptide dermal-regeneration blend of GHK-Cu, BPC-157, and TB-500. GHK-Cu upregulates dermal wound-healing genes and stimulates collagen and elastin; BPC-157 restores microvascular flow via FAK/AKT/eNOS and organizes collagen-fibril deposition; TB-500 regulates G-actin to drive cell migration and minimize rigid scar tissue.

Research focus

Skin aging, photo-damage, post-procedure dermal recovery (microneedling, laser resurfacing, chemical peels), and acute musculoskeletal trauma.

Supporting clinical evidence

  • In placebo-controlled trials, GHK-Cu creams significantly increased skin thickness, density, and elasticity while reducing coarse and fine wrinkles.
  • In rodent full-thickness wound models, TB-500 (Thymosin β4) increased re-epithelialization and enhanced collagen deposition and neovascularization.
  • A synthetic actin-binding heptapeptide (LKKTETQ) promoted dermal repair in diabetic and senescent mice at rates comparable to the parent molecule.
Longevity

MOTS-c

Mechanism of action

A 16-amino-acid mitochondrial-derived peptide that acts as both an endocrine signal and a nuclear transcriptional regulator. Under metabolic stress it translocates to the nucleus in an AMPK-dependent manner, binds antioxidant-response-element transcription factors, and in skeletal muscle activates AMPK/Akt to upregulate GLUT4 and promote insulin-dependent glucose clearance, while suppressing the MAPK/c-Fos and Nrf2/TXNIP/NLRP3 inflammatory axes.

Research focus

Insulin resistance, type-2 diabetes, metabolic syndrome, obesity, age-related sarcopenia, endurance decline, and degenerative joint disease.

Supporting clinical evidence

  • High-intensity exercise raised skeletal-muscle MOTS-c 11.9-fold; endogenous levels are ~21–29% higher in young adults than in middle-aged and elderly individuals.
  • In aged mice, systemic MOTS-c reversed age-related insulin resistance and improved glucose tolerance; in high-fat-diet obesity it prevented weight gain without hepatotoxicity.
  • In gestational-diabetes and osteoarthritis models it protected pancreatic β-cells and slowed cartilage degeneration via reduced NLRP3-mediated pyroptosis.
Longevity

NAD+ (Nicotinamide Adenine Dinucleotide)

Mechanism of action

A classical redox coenzyme for glycolysis, the TCA cycle, and oxidative phosphorylation, and an obligate cosubstrate for sirtuins (SIRT1–7), PARPs, and CD38. Sirtuins use NAD+ to deacetylate histones and transcription factors governing stress resistance, DNA repair, and circadian rhythm. Tissue NAD+ declines with age as NAMPT falls and PARP1/CD38 activity rises.

Research focus

Age-related metabolic and vascular decline, peripheral artery disease, cognitive decline, neurodegeneration (Alzheimer's, Parkinson's, Huntington's), cardiac hypertrophy, and skeletal-muscle atrophy.

Supporting clinical evidence

  • Pilot 4-week trial NCT06534944 (Nicotinamide Riboside in peripheral artery disease) improved peripheral endothelial and cerebrovascular function and cognitive testing, reducing oxidative stress and raising nitric-oxide production.
  • Preclinically, inhibiting PARP1 or CD38 elevated NAD+ and restored SIRT1/SIRT3 activity, protecting against metabolic decline and cardiac ischemic damage.
  • NAD+ precursors in Alzheimer's models restored mitochondrial function, upregulated PGC-1α, and reduced toxic amyloid-β accumulation.

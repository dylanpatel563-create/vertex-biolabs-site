Research & Quality

The science behind the bottle

We build every formula on credible evidence, then prove what’s inside with independent testing. Here’s how we do it.

Our approach

Evidence first, marketing second

We start every product with the research. An ingredient only earns a place in a Vertex blend if there’s credible, peer-reviewed work behind it — and we use it at the doses studies actually rely on, not pixie-dusted trace amounts that look good on a label.

We read the literature critically: study size, design, funding, and whether results hold up across independent trials. When the evidence is early or mixed, we say so rather than overpromise.

The result is a short, honest list of ingredients you can look up yourself — and ask us about anytime.

How we test

From raw material to your shelf

Every batch passes through the same four checkpoints before it ships.

1

Source & vet

We qualify each supplier and request identity and origin documentation for every raw ingredient.

2

Identity & potency

Incoming materials are tested to confirm they are what they claim — and as potent as they should be.

3

Purity screen

Finished batches are screened by an independent lab for heavy metals, microbes, and contaminants.

4

Certificate of analysis

Each batch gets a COA tied to its lot number — available to you on request, anytime.

Good to know

Research, answered

Do you run your own clinical trials?

Not yet. We formulate based on published, peer-reviewed research on individual ingredients, and we verify our finished products with independent third-party labs. We’re transparent about which claims are well-established and which are still emerging.

What does “third-party tested” actually mean here?

It means an independent lab — not us — tests each batch for identity, potency, and contaminants like heavy metals and microbes. The results are recorded on a certificate of analysis tied to that batch’s lot number.

Are your products FDA approved?

Dietary supplements are not approved by the FDA. Our products are made in line with FDA dietary-supplement regulations, and our statements have not been evaluated by the FDA. They are not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease.

Where can I read the studies myself?

We’re happy to point you to the research behind any ingredient — just ask. We encourage looking ingredients up on resources like PubMed and bringing questions to your healthcare provider.
